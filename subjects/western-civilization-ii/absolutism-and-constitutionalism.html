Western Civilization II
Absolutism and Constitutionalism, 1648–1715

Overview

The period from 1648 to 1715 saw the rise of absolutist monarchies and constitutional states across Europe. These competing models of governance reshaped political power, influenced state-building, and laid the foundation for modern nation-states. The balance between royal authority and legal limits was central to European political development in this era.

Key Themes and Developments

  • The Dutch Republic:
    • A unique case of republican government in a monarchical age.
    • Economic prosperity through trade, finance, and naval power.
    • Religious toleration and flourishing of Dutch Golden Age culture.
  • The English Revolution:
    • English Civil War (1642–1651): Conflict between monarchy and Parliament.
    • Oliver Cromwell: Military dictatorship under the Commonwealth.
    • Restoration (1660): Return of monarchy under Charles II.
    • Glorious Revolution (1688): William and Mary invited to rule under constitutional limits — resulted in the English Bill of Rights (1689).
  • France under Louis XIV:
    • Absolutism personified: Centralized power, Versailles as symbol of royal control.
    • Mercantilism: Colbert's economic policies strengthened royal revenue.
    • Religious uniformity: Revocation of the Edict of Nantes (1685) suppressed Protestantism.
  • Austrian and Prussian State Formation:
    • Austria: Multiethnic Habsburg empire consolidated control through dynastic marriages and wars.
    • Prussia: Militarized bureaucracy and centralized monarchy under Frederick William (“the Great Elector”).
  • Russia’s Westernization:
    • Peter the Great: Modernized military, reformed government, built St. Petersburg as new capital.
    • Reduced power of nobility, established control over the Orthodox Church.

Quick Tip

Absolutism and constitutionalism represent two paths toward centralized authority. Focus on how monarchs consolidated power — or how legal structures checked it — and how these models shaped modern statecraft and revolution.
