Principles of Marketing
Pricing Strategies

Overview

Price captures value in exchange; it influences demand, profitability, and market position. Effective pricing blends cost, customer perception, and competitive dynamics while aligning with company objectives.

Key Concepts

  • Cost-Based Pricing: Cost-plus markup, break-even analysis, target return.
  • Value-Based Pricing: Price set on perceived customer value; uses economic value to customer (EVC).
  • Competition-Based Pricing: Going-rate, loss leader, price matching.
  • New-Product Strategies: Skimming (high initial price) vs. penetration (low to gain share).
  • Psychological Pricing: Odd–even ($19.99), reference price framing, decoy effect.
  • Dynamic & Yield Management: Real-time adjustment based on demand (airlines, ride-hailing).
  • Bundling & Freemium: Package offerings or free basic tier with paid upgrades.
  • Legal & Ethical Issues: Price fixing, predatory pricing, deceptive practices, resale price maintenance.

Step-by-Step Example

Scenario: StreamPlay launches a cloud‑gaming subscription.

Step 1 – Costs: Service cost per user = $6/month (servers, royalties).

Step 2 – Value: Survey shows gamers willing to pay up to $15 for unlimited titles.

Step 3 – Competition: Rivals priced at $9.99 and $16.99 tiers.

Step 4 – Price Choice: Use penetration strategy at $7.99 promo for 6 months, then $11.99.

Step 5 – Psychological: Anchor premium bundle at $17.99 to make $11.99 tier appear value.

Final Answer: Combining cost, value, and competitive reference points delivers profit margin and rapid adoption.

Quick Tip

Run A/B pricing tests online—small sample experiments reveal demand elasticity before nationwide rollouts.
