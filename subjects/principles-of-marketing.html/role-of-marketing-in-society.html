Principles of Marketing
Role of Marketing in Society

Overview

Marketing shapes the flow of goods, services, and ideas that satisfy human needs. Beyond driving profits, marketing influences cultural trends, job creation, and societal well‑being. This topic examines how marketing contributes value to consumers, organizations, and the broader economy—while highlighting ethical responsibilities.

Key Concepts and Terms

  • Marketing’s Macro Role: Links producers and consumers through exchange, stimulates competition, and allocates resources efficiently.
  • Societal Marketing Concept: Balances company profits, consumer satisfaction, and society’s long‑run interests (e.g., sustainable packaging).
  • Ethical Marketing: Truthful promotion, privacy protection, fair pricing, and responsible targeting (e.g., avoiding greenwashing).
  • Nonprofit Marketing: Uses segmentation and positioning to attract donors, volunteers, and beneficiaries—value measured in mission impact.
  • International Marketing: Adapts product, price, place, and promotion to different cultures, economic stages, and legal systems.
  • Macro‑Environment Trends: Demographic shifts, social justice movements, digital technology, and climate concerns reshape marketing priorities.

Step‑by‑Step Example

Scenario: PureSip launches a biodegradable water bottle. How does societal marketing guide its strategy?

Step 1 – Consumer Need: On‑the‑go hydration without plastic guilt.

Step 2 – Company Profit: Premium price offsets higher material cost.

Step 3 – Societal Benefit: Bottle composts in 90 days, reducing landfill waste.

Step 4 – 4 P’s: Product (plant‑based polymer), Price ($1.69), Place (eco‑friendly retailers), Promotion (educational TikTok videos).

Final Answer: By integrating consumer value, competitive profit, and environmental stewardship, PureSip exemplifies the societal marketing concept.

Quick Tip

Consumers reward authenticity—back up social‑impact claims with third‑party certifications like B Corp or Fair Trade to build trust.
