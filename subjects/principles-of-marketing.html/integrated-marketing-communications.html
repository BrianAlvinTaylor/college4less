Principles of Marketing
Integrated Marketing Communications

Overview

Integrated Marketing Communications (IMC) blends advertising, public relations, sales promotion, social media, and personal selling into a consistent voice. An IMC strategy ensures every touchpoint delivers a unified message and amplifies brand impact.

Key Concepts

  • Promotion Mix Elements: Advertising, PR, sales promotion, personal selling, direct & digital marketing.
  • AIDA Model: Attention → Interest → Desire → Action guides message design.
  • Push vs. Pull: Push targets channel partners; pull targets end consumers to create demand.
  • Message Consistency: Brand story, tone, visuals, and value proposition aligned across media.
  • Media Planning: Reach, frequency, GRPs, CPM; paid–owned–earned media framework.
  • IMC Budget Methods: Percentage of sales, competitive parity, objective-and-task (preferred).
  • Measurement: ROMI (return on marketing investment), engagement metrics, attribution models.

Step-by-Step Example

Scenario: SolarSip launches a solar-powered iced-tea brewer and needs an IMC plan.

1 Objective & Task: Goal: Sell 10,000 units in 6 months. Tasks: build awareness, educate, convert.

2 Message Platform: "Brew sunshine into every cup—zero electricity." Tone bright & eco-friendly.

3 Mix:

  • Advertising: 15s YouTube pre‑roll, Instagram Reels.
  • PR: Earth Day press release, influencer unboxings.
  • Sales Promotion: $20 launch rebate + referral credits.
  • Personal Selling: Live demos at farmers' markets.
  • Email/Digital: Welcome series with how‑to videos.

4 Schedule: Teaser ads → launch week blitz → sustain with user‑generated content.

5 Measurement: Track unique discount codes, site conversions, and brand‑lift survey.

Final Answer: An IMC plan syncing paid, owned, and earned channels builds cohesive brand narrative and drives SolarSip sales.

Quick Tip

Apply content calendars—map messages across channels and dates to avoid mixed signals and optimize timing.
