Topics Covered
  • History of psychology and major theoretical approaches
  • Biological, cognitive, psychodynamic, behavioral, humanistic, and biopsychosocial models
  • Research methods: experimental, correlational, clinical, and ethical guidelines
  • Functional organization of the nervous system and neuroanatomy
  • Endocrine system, genetics, and physiological techniques
  • Sensation and perception including thresholds and sensory adaptation
  • Vision, audition, and other senses: olfaction, gustation, somesthesis, and vestibular
  • Sleep, dreaming, psychoactive drugs, hypnosis, and meditation
  • Classical and operant conditioning, reinforcement, observational learning
  • Cognition: memory, language, thinking, problem-solving, and creativity
  • Motivation and emotion: theories and biological bases
  • Social motivation, stress, anxiety, and self-determination theory
  • Developmental theories and lifespan development: cognitive, moral, social
  • Gender identity, sex roles, heredity-environment interaction
  • Personality: major theories, self-esteem, and assessment techniques
  • Psychological disorders: anxiety, depression, somatoform, dissociative, and personality disorders
  • Therapies: behavioral, cognitive, biological, insight, and preventive models
  • Social psychology: attitudes, attribution, prejudice, group behavior, and prosocial action
  • Statistics and testing: descriptive and inferential statistics, validity, and reliability
  • Testing norms, scales, types of assessments, and intelligence measurement
