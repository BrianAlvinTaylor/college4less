Foundations of American Democracy

Overview

The foundations of American democracy are rooted in Enlightenment ideas about natural rights, popular sovereignty, and the social contract. These principles shaped the Declaration of Independence and the U.S. Constitution, forming the basis for the American political system.

Key Themes and Events

  • Natural Rights: Inspired by John Locke, these include life, liberty, and property, which governments are created to protect.
  • Social Contract: The idea that government power derives from the consent of the governed.
  • Popular Sovereignty: The authority of the government comes from the people.
  • Declaration of Independence (1776): Articulated grievances against the British crown and established the United States as a free nation.
  • Influence of Enlightenment Thinkers: Philosophers like Montesquieu and Rousseau shaped American ideals about liberty and governance.

Quick Tip

Focus on how Enlightenment principles were embedded into founding documents. Practice identifying where concepts like natural rights and popular sovereignty appear in historical texts.
