Education Reform and Public Policy

Exploring how education policy has shaped opportunity and inequality in America

Overview

Education reform reflects the nation's evolving views on citizenship, opportunity, and equity. From the expansion of public schooling to modern debates over standardized testing and school choice, education policy has played a major role in shaping American society.

Impact and Controversies

While education reforms aim to promote access and excellence, they often spark debate over local control, funding, curriculum standards, and racial or economic disparities. The balance between federal and state oversight remains a central issue.

Study Focus

Know major legislation, court cases, and shifts in public education. Understand how educational changes have influenced civic life, economic mobility, and social equality.
