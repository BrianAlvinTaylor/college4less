U.S. Foreign Policy and Global Conflicts

Examining America's role in world affairs and international conflict

Overview

U.S. foreign policy has evolved from isolationism to global leadership. The 20th and 21st centuries saw the United States play central roles in two world wars, the Cold War, and conflicts in the Middle East, shaping global politics and economic systems.

Impact and Controversies

Foreign interventions have sparked debates over American values, military spending, and international cooperation. While securing influence, the U.S. has also faced criticism over human rights, unilateralism, and long-term consequences of military action.

Study Focus

Understand key doctrines, military conflicts, and diplomatic shifts. Pay attention to Cold War strategy, the War on Terror, and how foreign policy reflects domestic concerns and global ambitions.
