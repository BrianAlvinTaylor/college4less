Civil Rights and Social Movements

Exploring the fight for justice, equality, and social change in modern America

Overview

Civil rights and social justice movements have profoundly shaped American society. From the post-Reconstruction struggles of Black Americans to women's liberation, LGBTQ+ rights, and modern activism, these movements have expanded democracy and challenged structural inequalities.

  • Chicano, Native American, and Asian American civil rights movements
  • Modern activism: Black Lives Matter, #MeToo, March for Our Lives

Challenges and Achievements

While progress has been made in legislation and social attitudes, civil rights remain a dynamic and ongoing struggle. Key victories include desegregation, voting rights expansion, workplace protections, and greater public representation.

Study Focus

Students preparing for U.S. History II should understand the chronology, goals, leaders, and outcomes of major civil rights movements. Connections between historical and contemporary activism are also important.
