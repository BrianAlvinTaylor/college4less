Polynomial and Rational Equations

Overview

This topic focuses on solving higher-degree polynomial equations and rational equations. You'll learn how to factor complex polynomials, find real and complex solutions, and identify domain restrictions for rational expressions.

Key Concepts and Techniques

  • Polynomial Degree: The highest power of the variable determines the degree and the number of possible roots.
  • Factoring: Use factoring techniques such as grouping, synthetic division, and the Rational Root Theorem.
  • Zero Product Property: Used to solve polynomial equations after factoring.
  • Rational Equations: Equations involving rational expressions. Multiply by the least common denominator (LCD) to eliminate fractions.
  • Excluded Values: Values that make any denominator zero must be excluded from the solution set.
  • Extraneous Solutions: Always check for values that may solve the equation algebraically but are not valid due to restrictions.

Step-by-Step Example

Problem: Solve the rational equation: \frac{x}{x - 2} = \frac{3}{x + 2}

Step 1: Cross multiply.
x(x + 2) = 3(x - 2)

Step 2: Expand both sides.
x^2 + 2x = 3x - 6

Step 3: Move all terms to one side.
x^2 - x + 6 = 0

Step 4: Use the quadratic formula:
x = \frac{1 \pm \sqrt{(-1)^2 - 4(1)(6)}}{2(1)} = \frac{1 \pm \sqrt{-23}}{2}

Answer: No real solution (complex roots)

Quick Tip

When solving rational equations, always check your solution against the original equation. Values that make any denominator zero must be excluded!
